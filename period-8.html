Period 8

1945 - 1980
World War II ended with some unresolved issues and tensions, especially between the US and the USSR. Americans were very afraid of the growing threat of communism in the western world. This fear would eventually lead to the Cold War that lasted for years between the United States and Russia. The Vietnam War and Korean War also broke out as a result of the Cold War and tensions between the US and USSR. Americans became more and more fearful of communism, so the domino theory came about, which said that if one nation were to fall to communism, other countries would follow suit. The domino theory is what brought into the conflict in Vietnam. However, many Americans protested against US involvement in the Vietnam War. The Cold War wasn’t just a war where the US and USSR showed off their nuclear arms; they fought in a space race as well. Improvements in space technology eventually led to an American victory when Neil Armstrong became the first man on the moon. But that didn’t end the Cold War. Tensions between the United States and Russia kept rising with things like the kitchen debates between Nixon and Khrushchev, the U2 incident, and the Cuban Missile Crisis. Eventually a hotline was created between the White House and the Kremlin as a result of the Cuban Missile Crisis, which was the closest the two countries got to war. This time period is also characterized by the civil rights movement. Blac rights leaders such as Martin Luther King Jr. and Malcolm X led the civil rights movement and organizations like the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC) and the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee (SNCC) were created. Supreme Court cases like Brown v. Board of Education and the desegregation of the military showed progress during this time period. Then, in line with the previous Progressive movement, Lyndon B. Johnson’s Great Society focused on welfare and government aid for the poor and disabled. After that came Richard Nixon, who was more conservative but was eventually impeached due to the Watergate Scandal.

Synthesis
McCarthyism refers to the time period in the early 1950s, when Senator McCarthy used his position in Congress to baselessly accuse high-ranking government officials and other Americans of conspiracy with communism. The Salem Witch Trials were a series of witchcraft trials launched from 1692 to 1693 after a group of young girls in Salem, Massachusetts, claimed to have been bewitched by some of the older women in the colony. Both of these are examples of when baseless accusations were made, causing innocent people to be accused of being something they weren't and causing paranoia.

Brown v. Board of Education was a 1954 Supreme Court ruling that reversed the policy of segregation and declared that "separate can never be equal." Muller v. Oregon was a Supreme Court Case that upheld Oregon state restrictions on the working hours of women. Both of these Supreme Court cases show instances when the court looked outside the law to side with those being mistreated.

War hawks during the War of 1812 were Western settlers who advocated war with Britain because they hoped to acquire Britain's northwest posts and because they felt the British were aiding the Indians and encouraging them to attack the Americans on the frontier. Hawks during the Vietnam War were people who supported the Vietnam War despite the mass protests against the war. Both the war hawks in 1812 and the hawks during the Vietnam war advocated for war.
