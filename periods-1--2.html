Periods 1 & 2

1491 - 1754
Most people today think that American history started with European explorer Christopher Columbus’ accidental discovery of North America in 1492 while looking for a route to India, but Native Americans occupied the land for thousands of years before European arrival. It is believed that the first inhabitants of North America were Asians that went across the Bering Strait about 40,000 years ago. Over time, many unique cultures and native civilizations formed, focused on hunting and farming. During the Age of Exploration, Christopher Columbus’ discovery of America would later greatly impact not only these unique native societies, but civilizations all across the world as well. By exchanging things like plants, animals, and weapons from their lands, Columbus’ voyage brought the eastern and western hemispheres together. Known as the Columbian exchange, this trade between the New World and the Old World had long-lasting impacts and cemented a relationship between the two sides of the world. Around the time of Columbus’ discovery of America, Europeans were already exploring the world, mainly due to religious conflicts and improvements in technology. The Spanish and the Portuguese were the first to start exploring. The French and the Dutch followed, and then the English followed them. Fueled by the prospect of riches, glory, and religious freedom in the New World, Europeans started monetizing off of newly discovered lands establishing colonies, some taking over native lands and even killing the natives in doing so. Colonists of the New World experienced failure at first, as seen by England’s failure with so-called Lost Colony of Roanoke Island, but eventually successful settlements, like the Spanish settlement of St. Augustine and the English settlement of Jamestown, were created in the New World. Differences in goals, religious beliefs, location, and the types of people that settled created distinct differences in the 13 original English colonies and affected their development. At the time, despite their differences, the colonies were all still loyal to their mother country, England. Later on, rising tensions between the colonists and the British would change the course of history forever.

Mayflower Compact (1620)
  • Historical Context: 
    • Colonization
    • Plymouth Colony
  • Purpose:
    • To serve as an agreement for self-government that was to be enforced in the Americas
  • Point of View: 
    • Pro-democracy
Pope Alexander VI's
​Demarcation Bull ​(May 4, 1493)
  • Historical Context:
    • ​​Christopher Columbus' discovery of the New World
  • Purpose:
    • To support Spain’s strategy to ensure its exclusive right to the lands discovered by Columbus the previous year
  • Point of View:
    • ​​Pro-Spain
    • Supported colonization
Sebastian Brandt to Henry Hovener (January 13, 1622)
  • Historical Context:
    • Jamestown
    • Colonization
  • Purpose:
    • To describe life in Virginia
    • To put in orders for cheese, vinegar, tools, spices, and other assorted goods from the London Company that were not available in Virginia
      • And paying for those orders in tobacco and furs, not gold and copper
  • Point of View: 
    • Jamestown settler
John Winthrop to Nathaniel Rich (May 22, 1634)
  • Historical Context:
    • Boston
    • Colonization
  • Purpose:
    • To give Europeans a glimpse of what life was like in the early Boston settlement
  • Point of View: 
    • Boston settler

Turning Points
1
Columbian Exchange
  • Causes:
    • ​Columbus' discovery of the New World
  • Description:
    • ​Exchange between Americas, Europe, and Africa that included the trade of plants, animals, technology, ideas, and diseases
      • Introduced to Old World: Turkey, corn, potato, sweet potato, cocoa, tomatoes, syphilis, tobacco
      • Introduced to New World: Chicken, cows, apples, bananas, peaches, celery, mango, radish, a butt ton of diseases
  • Effects:
    • ​Irish become dependent on potatoes as a major source of income
    • Native American population declined rapidly
    • Represents a mixture of the new and the old
2
Treaty of Tordesillas
  • Causes:
    • ​Resolution concerning the border and land of the Spanish and Portuguese
  • Description:
    • ​A treaty drawing a line dividing South America to settle the land dispute
      • Portugal got what was to the right of the line
        • Modern-day Brazil
      • Spain got what was left of the line
        • Most of everything else
  • Effects:
    • ​Present-day Brazil speaks Portuguese
    • The rest of South America speaks Spanish
    • Conflict between Catholics and Protestants in South America
    • Settled major land dispute
3
Protestant Reformation
  • Causes:
    • ​Martin Luther didn't agree with the Catholic church's strict and corrupt type of ruling, especially with indulgences and selling forgiveness
    • He posted the "95 Theses," which many consider to be the start of the Protestant Reformation
  • Description:
    • ​Began when Martin Luther broke away from the church and posted the "95 Theses," a document criticizing the Catholic church
    • A movement to purge the church of all corruption and focus on the Bible instead of blindly following tradition
    • Luther made use of the printing press and utilized it to create a larger follower base
  • Effects:
    • ​Church broke apart from Catholics to Protestants
    • Lutherism became a religion
    • John Calvin followed the movement and also broke away from the church
    • Challenged religious views and leaders
    • Inspiration for democracy
4
Jamestown Founded
  • Causes:
    • ​Charted by the London Company
    • They believed there would be gold on the east coast and sent for people to find gold
  • Description:
    • ​Initially incredibly unsuccessful
      • They arrived during a drought and contracted many diseases
    • Mainly men settled here
    • Major disputes with the Powhatan Native Americans, who eventually befriended the colonists after a run in with John Smith
  • Effects:
    • ​1 of first permanent colonies in Americas
    • Major center for tobacco
    • Influenced other people to colonize America
5
Fundamental Orders of Connecticut Created
  • Causes:
    • ​Connecticut wanted to form a government
  • Description:
    • ​Like a modern day constitution
    • Set up the first strictly democratic government
      • Legislative branch that was elected by popular vote
      • Government appointed by members of the legislature
  • Effects:
    • ​Led to a more democratic government
    • Help set up democratic ideals

Synthesis
The First Great Awakening​​ was a sudden outbreak of religious fervor that swept through the colonies during the late 1730s and early 1740s. The Second Great Awakening was a series of religious revivals starting in 1801, based on Methodism and Baptism, that fueled the movements during the antebellum period. Both Great Awakenings were based on religion, showed more liberalism, and were unifying movements.

During the age of exploration and colonial times, Europeans explored and colonized the New World, thinking it would be better there and hoping to escape from things like religious persecution in Europe. African Americans migrated to the North during the Great Migration because they thought it would be better than the South. Both involved the mass migration of people who thought they'd live better lives elsewhere.

Bartolome de Las Casas spoke out against the atrocities by the Spanish against Native Americans. Harriet Beecher Stowe wrote Uncle Tom's Cabin to show the terrible atrocities committed against African American slaves. Both Bartolome de Las Casas and Harriet Beecher Stowe fought against injustice towards certain groups of people and spoke out about it.
