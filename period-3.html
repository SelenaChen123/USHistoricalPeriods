Period 3

1754 - 1800
The time period from the 1750s to 1800 saw huge changes. The First Great Awakening during the early/mid 18th century united the colonies with a religious awakening. The Age of Enlightenment during that time period saw the creation of major ideas such as checks and balances, the separation of church and state, the idea of natural laws, the idea of social contract, and the principles of life, liberty, and property. Both of these events would eventually lead to the fight against Britain during the American Revolution. The 13 colonies were originally loyal to their mother country, having fought alongside the British in many conflicts against natives and other European powers. One of the biggest conflicts that happened during this time period was the French and Indian War, that started over land disputes. The colonists and the British joined forces to fight against the French and the Native Americans. Although the colonists and the British won the war, it would drastically affect British-colonial relations. The first of many things that negatively affected British-colonial relations was the Proclamation Act of 1763, which said that colonists weren’t allowed to expand west of the Appalachian Mountains. This angered the colonists, since they just fought a war so they could settle on that land. Salutary neglect ended with the end of the French and Indian War, as the British needed to find ways to pay for the French and Indian War that they just fought. Britain started imposing taxes on the colonies and created regulatory laws like the Sugar Act, Quartering Act, and Townshend Acts that restricted the freedom of the colonists and further angered them. In response to the colonists’ anger and protests, the British bore down harder with more regulatory acts. This would eventually lead to the American Revolution. Starting with Lexington and Concord and ending with Yorktown, the colonists surprisingly won the Revolutionary War. And thus begins the creation of the country as we know it today. How this newly created country was governed would become a hugely debated topic throughout the course of American history, as the issue of states rights versus federal power was brought into the spotlight when the Articles of Confederation failed and the US Constitution was created. Partisan politics would eventually start to emerge during this time as well, even though George Washington warned against them during his Farewell Address. The first three presidential terms from Washington through Jefferson would see debates about the foreign and domestic policies in the new, growing nation.

1
Proclamation Act of 1763
  • Causes:
    • ​The French and Indian War
  • Description:
    • ​Colonists weren't allowed to settle west of the Appalachian Mountains for fear of another war with Native Americans
  • Effects:
    • ​Led to the American Revolution
    • Colonists started getting mad at Britain
2
Intolerable Acts Passed
  • Causes:
    • ​The Boston Tea Party
  • Description:
    • ​Composed of mainly 5 acts
      • Boston Port Act
        • Closed the Boston port
      • Massachusetts Government Act
        • A royal governor was installed in Massachusetts
      • Quartering Act
        • Housing of soldiers
      • Administration of Justice
        • Trials would be held in England for British officials
      • Quebec Act
        • Canadian border expanded
  • Effects:
    • ​First Continental Congress
    • Colonists got even more mad at Britain
    • Led to the American Revolution

  • Description:
    • ​Broke the ties between Britain and the colonists
    • Colonists declared their grievances
  • Effects:
    • ​The United States was formed
    • America gained its independence

Articles of Confederation Created
  • Causes:
    • ​​Revolutionary War
    • The need for a new form of government
  • Description:
    • ​Unicameral legislature that couldn't tax states
    • States only had 1 vote

    • To pass a law, 9 out of the 13 states had to approve
    • An army couldn't be raised
  • Effects:
    • ​Too weak
    • Shay's Rebellion
    • Constitutional Convention

US Constitution Created

  • Description:

    • Eventually got ratified
  • Effects:
    • ​​Became the basis of America
    • Federalist and Anti-Federalists created

Synthesis
The Olive Branch Petition was a final offer of peace to Britain, agreeing to be loyal to the British government if it addressed their grievances. The Crittenden Compromise of 1860 was an attempt proposed by Senator Crittenden to prevent the Civil War. Both attempted to prevent war, but failed.

The Articles of Confederation was the first American constitution that established the United States as a loose confederation of states under a weak national Congress. The League of Nations was an international organization founded in 1919 to promote world peace and cooperation. Both were weak, idealistic, and ultimately failed and got replaced.

Revolutionaries refer to people during the time of the American Revolutionary War that wanted to separate from Britain and establish their own country.
Confederates refer to people during the time of the Civil War that wanted to separate from the Union and establish their own country. Both groups of people had conflict with those they were splitting from and therefore wanted to split and form their own countries.
