Period 9

1980 - Present
The beginning of the time period from 1980 and onwards starts off conservative, as shown by the moral majority and the new right. America became more focused on traditional family roles and religious values. Ronald Reagan was then elected because of this new conservative climate. Reaganomics, or Reagan’s idea of trickle-down economics, was a big part of Reagan’s economic policy. By supporting businesses and corporations, he believed it would then stimulate the economy. During Reagan’s administration, there were also a lot of important social changes, like in terms of the AIDS epidemic, the war on drugs, and the Supreme Court case New Jersey v. TLO. The Iranian hostage crisis also ended when Reagan became president. United States embassy workers were held captive for 444 days during Jimmy Carter’s presidency because of US support of the shah that was overthrown in the Iranian Revolution. However, another Iranian issue appeared when the Iran-Contra Affair happened. After Reagan’s presidency, the Cold War started coming to an end when the Soviet Union fell. George H.W. Bush became president after Reagan, running on a campaign of “No New Taxes.” During his presidency, the US entered the Gulf War. After that came Bill Clinton, who was most known for his affair with Monica Lewinsky that later led to his impeachment. Clinton was also well known for his debates against the Speaker of the House at the time, Newt Gingrich. Then came George W. Bush, who ran against Al Gore and on a campaign of “No Child Left Behind.” During Bush’s presidency, the 9/11 terrorist attack occurred, which eventually led to the creation of the Department of Homeland Security and the US Patriot Act. After Bush came the first African American president, Barack Obama. Under Obama’s administration, we see the implementation of the Affordable Care Act, also known as Obamacare. And finally, our current President is Donald Trump. Who knows what will happen to our country now?

Synthesis
Bill Clinton was impeached due to a sex scandal where he allegedly had sexual relations with a secretary named Monica Lewinsky. Richard Nixon was impeached due to the Watergate Scandal, where he tried to cover up his connection with the 5 men tried to break into the Democratic National Committee's offices prior to Nixon's re-election. In both of these instances, the American public lost trust in the presidency and Presidents were impeached, but never actually served jail time, as Clinton's offense wasn't actually punishable with jail time and Nixon resigned and was later pardoned by the president after him, Gerald Ford.

The "Don't Ask, Don't Tell" policy was meant to be a compromise that intended to restrict the US military from "witch-hunting" secretly gay, lesbian, and bisexual service members or applicants, while absolutely barring "openly" gay or bisexual people from joining the military and expelling those already serving during Clinton's term. The Gag Rule was a strict rule passed by pro-southern Congressmen in 1836 to prohibit all discussion of slavery in the House or Representatives. Both of these were meant to lessen tensions and ended up just stalling the inevitable.

The Affordable Care Act, also known as Obamacare, was passed to increase the quality and affordability of health insurance, lower the uninsured rate by expanding public and private insurance coverage, and reduce the costs of healthcare for individuals and the government. Medicaid was a program developed as a part of Lyndon B. Johnson's Great Society, and is a federally aided, state-operated program that provides medical benefits for low-income people in need of medical care. Both of these were designed to make health care and medical benefits more accessible for the average American and were more examples of reform.
