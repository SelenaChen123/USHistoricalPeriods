Period 7: Part 2

1920 - 1945
The beginning of the time period between 1920 and 1945 started off on a high note. 1920 began with the ratification of the 19th amendment, which gave women the right to vote. The fight for women’s suffrage during the Progressive era finally paid off. After that, the US saw a period of economic prosperity, during what is called the Roaring ‘20s. Improvements in technology, increased consumerism, and social changes shaped this time period. However, shortly afterwards, things just went downhill. With the election of Warren G. Harding, there came a period of political corruption again, with scandals like the Ohio Gang and the Teapot Dome scandal. There were already economic issues at the time, as seen by the mass closing of farms and such, but then the stock market crashed in 1929, starting a Great Depression. Herbert Hoover was the unfortunate president that served during this time, but he really didn't do anything to help stabilize the economy and fix things. He figured the situation would remedy itself, but it didn't. That's why Franklin D. Roosevelt won the presidency the next term. During his time as president, FDR expanded his power to create laws and programs that would help fix the economy. During his first 100 days as president, FDR held a special session with Congress to discuss plans for fixing America. During those first 100 days, FDR declared a bank holiday and passed the Glass Steagall Act, which helped to strengthen banks when they reopened. FDR’s domestic policy was called the New Deal, and was focused on three things: relief, reform, and recovery. With the New Deal programs, the government had a much bigger role in the average American life. Many different federal programs were implemented that are still in effect today. But after that, the US would soon be pulled into a second world war after Japan bombed Pearl Harbor. It's due to the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor and the growing anti-Japanese sentiment afterwards that Japanese-Americans were then wrongfully persecuted during World War II. Japanese-Americans were put into internment camps because Americans feared they were spies trying to help Japan. World War II finally ended with the bombing of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.

Synthesis
Consumerism in the 1920s, also known as the Roaring '20s, increased as we saw an increasing number of Americans buying things not only for subsistence, but also for luxury and pleasure. Consumerism in the 1950s also increased after World War II, as people amassed war-time savings and there was a new prosperity.  Both of these decades saw economic prosperity and increased consumerism.

US involvement in World War II started after Japan attacked Pearl Harbor and we joined the side of the Allies to fight against the Axis powers. US involvement in World War I started due to unrestricted submarine warfare and the Zimmerman Note. Both of these show that the US trying to remain neutral, but were brought into the world conflicts using force and won both times.
