Period 5

1844 - 1877
One of the most important eras in American history is the time period from 1844-1877. Beginning with the Mexican-American War, the US was caught up in a dispute against Mexico about the border of Texas. This war served as the training ground for the Civil War, and with our victory in the war, we got the Treaty of Guadalupe-Hidalgo and the Mexican Cession. However, as the US continued to expand and gain more territory, the North and South were at odds over which states and territories should allow slavery. The Missouri Compromise temporarily solved the slavery issue by establishing the 36o30’ line, but as the US continued to gain new territories to the north and south of that line, the issue continued to persist. Henry Clay proposed the Compromise of 1850 to try and solve the issue, but the US kept gaining new territories and tensions between the North and the South kept escalating. Popular sovereignty ended up being one of the go-to solutions to the slavery dispute, allowing the people that inhabited the new territories to decide whether they wanted to allow slavery or not. However, that didn’t work either. Tensions just kept rising, and at last, the Southern states seceded one by one when Abraham Lincoln became President. The Civil War began. Starting with the first shot at Fort Sumter and ending with Robert E. Lee’s surrender at Appomattox Courthouse, the Civil War  ended with a Union victory. After the war, we entered a period of reconstruction. Abraham Lincoln was lenient on the South and constructed a reconstruction plan focused on uniting the North and the South again, but Lincoln was assassinated just 5 days after the Civil War ended. Andrew Johnson took over for Lincoln and proposed his own Reconstruction plan that was also fairly lenient towards the South, but Congress and the Radical Republicans opposed his plan, wanting to push down on the South harder. Conflict over how the nation should be reconstructed were debated for a while, until Rutherford B. Hayes was elected during the Compromise of 1877.

Turning Points
1
Compromise of 1850
  • Causes:
    • ​Question of slavery with the addition of new states
  • Description:
    • ​​Included:
      • California's admittance as a free state
      • Texas giving up claimed and for $10 million
      • Division of the Mexican Cession
        • Resolution that whether or not those states would have slavery would be up to popular sovereignty
      • Fugitive Slave Act
  • Effects:
    • ​Introduced idea of popular sovereignty
    • Addition of California as a free state
    • DC's ending of the slave trade
    • Slavery above 36th parallel
    • Stalled the Civil War
2
Kansas-Nebraska Act
  • Causes:
    • ​​There was tension with the use of popular sovereignty to decide slave or free states
    • ​​Opportunity for the creation for eastern Transcontinental Railroad
  • Description:
    • ​Act that set up Kansas and Nebraska as states
    • Slavery would be decided with the use of popular sovereignty
  • Effects:
    • ​Ended the Compromise of 1850
    • Repealed Missouri Compromise
    • Bleeding Kansas
    • The Republican party was formed

Election of 1960
  • Causes:
    • ​​Just another election year
  • Description:
    • ​Lincoln won the presidency but with only 40% of the popular vote
    • Southern voters all supported the same candidate, but Lincoln won
  • Effects:
    • ​Caused South Carolina and other Southern states to secede and leave the Union
4
Battle of Vicksburg
  • Causes:
    • ​​Civil War
  • Description:
    • ​The Union wanted to divide the South through what they called the Anaconda Plan
    • General Grant led the Union forces in the Battle of Vicksburg
    • He defeated two Confederate armies and destroyed the city
    • Five days later they had complete control of the Mississippi
  • Effects:
    • ​​Helped turn the tide of the war in the North's favor by giving them control of the Mississippi
    • "Suffocated" the South by cutting off the Confederate supplies coming from the West
    • Helped lead to a Union victory in the war
5
Compromise of 1877
  • Causes:
    • ​Election of 1876
  • Description:
    • ​Determined winner of the presidential election of 1876
    • Rutherford B. Hayes would be president if he pulled the troops from the south
    • A Southern Democrat would be included in his cabinet
    • The transcontinental railroad would be constructed
  • Effects:
    • ​Hayes was elected president with electoral votes despite his candidate receiving the popular vote
    • Hayes fulfilled all of the promises of the compromise

Synthesis
Uncle Tom's Cabin was a novel written by Harriet Beecher Stowe that showed the horrors of slavery. Common Sense was a pamphlet written by Thomas Paine that argued that the colonists should free themselves from British rule and establish an independent government based on Enlightenment ideals. Both Uncle Tom's Cabin and Common Sense influenced the people greatly and have been credited with helping to push the American people to go to war (the Civil War and Revolutionary War, respectively).

South Carolina seceded from the Union after Abraham Lincoln won the election of 1860, because they a slave state and Lincoln was against slavery. The South Carolina Nullification Crisis was a sectional crisis during the presidency of Andrew Jackson where they declared that they had the power to nullify federal tariffs since they thought that the tariffs were unconstitutional. Both incidents involved South Carolina standing up against what they believed were infringements of their rights and taking brave steps to fight against what they believed was hurting them.
